The supreme god
In the Shaivite tradition, Shiva is the supreme god and a member of the Hindu triumvirti, along with Brahma and Vishnu. The destroyer Shiva is known as the destroyer of the world, but he also has many other roles, such as creator, restorer, ascetic, and symbol of sensuality. The yogic source In yogic culture, Shiva is considered the source of yogic traditions and a being who lived in the Himalayan region.
The patron god Shiva is the patron god of yoga, meditation, and the arts.The iconography Shiva is often depicted with a crescent moon, a third eye on his forehead, a trident, and a serpent king around his neck.
The benevolent and fierce Shiva has many aspects, including benevolent and fierce. In benevolent aspects, he is depicted as a householder with his wife Parvati and their two children, Ganesha and Kartikeya. In his fierce aspects, he is often depicted slaying demons. The worship Shiva is usually worshiped in the aniconic form of lingam. The story of Apasmara In one story, Shiva took the form of Nataraja to defeat the demon Apasmara, the symbol of ignorance. Shiva , or Siva, Major deity of Hinduism, believed to have many manifestations. Like Vishnu, he is the subject of an elaborate and sometimes contradictory mythology. He is both the destroyer and the restorer, the great ascetic and the symbol of sensuality, the benevolent herdsman of souls and the wrathful avenger.
Shiva and Parvati

are a couple in Hindu mythology who are a symbol of love, power, and devotion. Here are some things to know about them: Shiva Also known as Mahadeva, Shiva is the supreme god in Shaivism, a Hindu tradition. He is the destroyer god, who removes evil from the world. Shiva is also known as the Adhiguru, or first guru, because he took human form to guide humans.
PARVATHI
The goddess of love, fertility, and devotion, Parvati is often depicted as the feminine side of Shiva. She is also known as Devi Durga and is a manifestation of the Hindu goddess Shakti. Together Shiva and Parvati are a formidable couple. Shiva's power comes from helping people realize their true selves, while Parvati recreates what Shiva dissolves. Other aspects Shiva has many names, including Viswanatha, Mahandeo, Mahasu, Mahesha, Maheshvara, Shankara, Shambhu, Rudra, Hara, Trilochana, Devendra, Neelakanta, Subhankara, Trilokinatha, and Ghrneshwar. Family Shiva and Parvati live on Mount Kailash with their two sons, Kartikeya and Ganesha.
